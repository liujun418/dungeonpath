1. Introduction#

DungeonPath (“we,” “our,” or “us”), accessible at https://dungeonpath.com/, is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your personal information when you visit our website (the “Site”), in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and other applicable data protection laws.

By accessing or using the Site, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with any part of this policy, please discontinue use of the Site.

2.4 Data Processing Location#

All analytics data is processed by Google’s infrastructure (Google Analytics 4 and Google AdSense). DungeonPath itself does not operate independent servers that store or process raw user data beyond what is necessary for the Site to function. No user data leaves the user’s browser and is independently uploaded or stored to servers controlled directly by DungeonPath — all data collection and processing is handled client-side by the third-party services disclosed below.

3. Cookies and Tracking Technologies#

3.1 What Are Cookies#

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites you visit. They are widely used to make websites function efficiently and to provide information to the site operators. Cookies may be “persistent” (remain on your device until they expire or are deleted) or “session” (deleted when you close your browser).

3.3 Google AdSense DART Cookie#

The Site displays advertisements served by Google AdSense, a service provided by Google LLC (“Google”). Google and its third-party advertising partners use cookies — including the DART (Dynamic Advertising Reporting and Targeting) cookie — to serve ads based on your prior visits to our Site and/or other websites on the internet.

  • The DART cookie enables Google and its partners to serve interest-based advertisements to you based on your browsing activity across the web.
  • Google’s use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visit to our Site and/or other sites on the internet.
  • You may review Google’s Privacy Policy at https://policies.google.com/privacy.
  • You may view and manage your Google ad personalization settings at https://www.google.com/settings/ads.

3.4 Google Analytics (GA4)#

We use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to understand how visitors interact with the Site. GA4 collects anonymized usage data including:

  • Pages visited and navigation paths
  • Time spent on each page
  • Approximate geographic location (country/region level)
  • Device and browser characteristics
  • Referring website or search engine

GA4 does not store IP addresses in full — IP anonymization is enabled. GA4 does not use or share personally identifiable information. For more information, see Google’s Analytics Privacy & Security documentation.
