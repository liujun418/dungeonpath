Understanding the PS99 Trading Economy#

Pet Simulator 99 has one of the most robust player-driven economies on Roblox. Unlike most games where items are bought from NPCs, PS99 relies on player-to-player trading for the best pets, enchants, and items. Understanding this economy is essential for getting strong pets without spending real money.

The Value System#

Pets in PS99 are valued using two main metrics:

MetricDescription
RAP (Recent Average Price)The average price that a pet has recently traded for. Updated in real-time based on actual trades.
Gem/Diamond ValueThe actual currency value that players demand in trades. This can differ from RAP due to supply, demand, and hype.

Important: RAP is a reference point, not an absolute truth. Popular pets often trade above RAP, while flooded-market pets trade below it.

The same pet can have dramatically different values based on its variant:

Event-exclusive pets (limited-time eggs, seasonal events) also hold their value significantly better than standard pets because they cannot be obtained outside the event window.

Where and How to Trade#

Trading Plaza#

The Trading Plaza is the central hub for in-game trading, located in the Spawn World. Here you can:

  • Browse player booths — other players set up shops with pets for sale
  • Set up your own booth — display items you want to trade or sell
  • Initiate direct trades — approach another player and open a trade window

Personal Trades#

Direct player-to-player negotiations. These happen anywhere in the game world. You approach another player, click “Trade,” and both parties select what they are offering.

Traderie (External Marketplace)#

Traderie (traderie.com/pet-simulator99) is a community-moderated marketplace for PS99. Benefits include:

  • Verified transactions reduce scam risk
  • Price history charts
  • Searchable listings for specific pets
  • Ability to buy/sell for real money (at your own risk)

How to Get the Best Deals#

Rule 1: Know the Value Before You Trade#

Never enter a trade without checking the current RAP and value list for every pet involved. A pet you think is worth 1B gems might actually be worth 10B — or 100M.

Rule 2: Trade Up, Not Sideways#

The fastest way to build value is through a series of trades:

  1. Start with what you have (pets, enchants, potions)
  2. Trade for something slightly more valuable
  3. Repeat until you reach your target pet

Even small “trade ups” of 10-20% compound quickly.

Rule 3: Buy During Downtime#

Pet values fluctuate with:

  • New updates — new pets flood the market, lowering existing pet values
  • Events — event-exclusive pets spike during the event, then drop afterward
  • Weekends — more players online means more competition and higher prices
  • After school hours — the player base surges, driving up demand

The best time to trade is during weekdays, right after a new update when prices are in flux.

Rule 4: Sell What You Do Not Need#

Every potion, flag, fruit, enchant, and duplicate pet has value. Sell items you do not need in the Trading Plaza to accumulate gems for bigger purchases.

Scam Prevention Guide#

Common Scams to Watch For#

Scam TypeHow It WorksHow to Avoid
Fake RAPScammer shows you a fake value screenAlways check values yourself using the tools listed above
Quick SwitchScammer swaps the pet at the last secondDouble-check the trade window before accepting
“Trust Trade”Scammer asks you to give your pet first, promises to send theirs afterNever trust trust trades. Use simultaneous exchange only
Wrong VariantScammer trades a Normal pet when you agreed on a Rainbow oneVerify the variant (Golden, Rainbow, Shiny) in the trade window
Fake MiddlemanSomeone pretends to be a trusted middlemanOnly use verified middlemen from official Discord servers
Overpay LureScammer offers way too much to make you excited, then changes the dealIf it seems too good, it probably is. Stay calm and verify

Red Flags#

  • The trade partner is rushing you to accept
  • They refuse to let you check values
  • They offer a “special deal” that you need to decide on immediately
  • They ask you to trade outside the Trading Plaza
  • The pet they are offering has an unusually low RAP for its rarity

What to Do If You Get Scammed#

Unfortunately, BIG Games does not restore scammed items. Your best options are:

  1. Report the player through Roblox’s reporting system
  2. Share the scammer’s username on community Discords to warn others
  3. Learn from the experience and be more careful next time

Frequently Asked Questions#

How do I trade in Pet Simulator 99?#

Go to the Trading Plaza in the Spawn World. You can browse player booths, set up your own shop, or initiate direct trades with other players. For external trading, use Traderie (traderie.com/pet-simulator99).

What does RAP mean?#

RAP stands for Recent Average Price. It tracks the average price that pets have recently traded for in actual player trades. Use it as a reference point to determine if a trade is fair.

How do I avoid scams when trading?#

Always check live RAP values before every trade. Never do “trust trades.” Verify pet variants in the trade window before accepting. If a deal seems too good to be true, it probably is a scam.

What are the most valuable pets right now?#

Gargantuan pets are the most valuable tier, followed by Titanic pets. As of May 2026, the Titanic Persimmony Cricket (~700B gems), Titanic Valkyrie Pegasus (~535B), and Titanic Easter Golem (~498B) are among the most valuable pets.
