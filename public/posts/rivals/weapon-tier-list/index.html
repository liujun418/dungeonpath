Understanding Weapon Stats#

Before diving into tier rankings, here’s what each stat means:

StatWhat It DoesTarget Value
DamageBase damage per hit/bulletHigher = faster time-to-kill (TTK)
Fire RateTime between shotsLower = faster (0.10s is very fast)
AmmoMagazine capacity / total reserveLarger clips = less reload pressure
Move SpeedHow the weapon affects your movementPositive = faster, negative = slower
Crit MultiplierMultiplier on headshots/critical hitsHigher = more burst damage
Range DropoffDamage reduction at distanceNo dropoff = consistent at all ranges

Important: Damage dropoff is the hidden stat that makes or breaks weapons. A weapon with 50 damage at close range but heavy dropoff at 50+ studs performs very differently than one with consistent damage.

Primary Weapons Tier Rankings#

S+ Tier — The Meta Kings#

These weapons dominate the current meta. If you can afford them, buy them immediately.

WeaponDamageFire RateAmmoMove SpeedPriceHow to Get
Permafrost14 per bullet0.30s (2-round burst)24/72Normal700 KeysShop (was Season 2 Prime Pass exclusive)

Permafrost — The Undisputed #1: The Permafrost is the single best weapon in RIVALS. Its 2-round burst fire at 14 damage per bullet delivers devastating mid-range TTK, and the unique freeze/slow effect (up to -50% movement speed) locks down opponents, preventing escape. Even after Season 3 adjustments, it remains dominant because the movement impairment utility is unmatched among primaries. It’s the cornerstone of every competitive loadout.

Paintball Gun — Consistency King: With 22 damage per shot and the fastest practical fire rate among primaries, the Paintball Gun rewards good tracking aim. It excels at mid-range where most fights happen.

Sniper — The Long-Range Specialist: 50 base damage with a x3.00 crit multiplier means headshots are instant kills. The -20% move speed penalty is the tradeoff — you’re vulnerable up close. Pair with a mobile melee like Scythe.

Burst Rifle — Budget Powerhouse: At just 25 Keys, the Burst Rifle delivers high burst damage for close-mid range fights. Note: it was nerfed in the Lucky Update (March 2026) — body damage dropped from 20 to 18. Still excellent value.

Assault Rifle — The Free Meta: The best free weapon in the game. With 12 damage at 0.10s fire rate and a generous 20/100 ammo pool, it’s reliable at all ranges. The Season 3 update actually buffed its fire rate from 0.11s to 0.10s, offsetting the damage nerf (13→12).

Flamethrower — Corridor Crusher: 85 DPS continuous damage with 25 afterburn damage and 26-stud reach. On close-quarters maps like Backrooms, this is absolutely devastating. The -5% move speed is minimal.

Crossbow — The Mobile Sniper: No damage dropoff means it hits just as hard at 200 studs as at point-blank. The +5% move speed bonus makes it the fastest-moving primary. Essentially a lightweight sniper with better mobility.

Minigun — Angle Holder: 300 ammo pool and insane sustained DPS after the 0.80s windup. Best for holding angles on open maps. The -25% move speed is punishing — you’re a sitting duck while spinning up.

RPG — Area Denial Specialist: 50 damage with a 16-stud splash radius. Great for flushing enemies out of cover and movement tech (grenade boosting). The 0.25s cooldown is surprisingly fast for a rocket launcher.

Shotgun — Close-Range Terror: 75 max damage up close (7.5 x 10 pellets), dropping to 25 at range. The range limitation is brutal — beyond 10 studs, effectiveness plummets. Best on small maps or as a complement to a long-range primary.

Bow — High Skill Ceiling: Changed from hitscan to projectile weapon in Update 20. Charge multipliers adjusted from 50/100/150/240% to 100/125/150/200% — more consistent but lower peak damage. Requires leading shots and timing charges.

Revolver — Burst Punisher: 30 damage at close range dropping to 8. The fan cooldown (0.15s) allows rapid follow-up shots. Great for punishing enemies who miss their primary.

Scythe — The Aggressive King: Best mobility melee in RIVALS. The dash ability (4s cooldown) enables unmatched aggressive plays — close gaps instantly, dodge incoming fire, and reposition mid-fight.

For a complete guide to movement mechanics and weapon synergies with speed modifiers, see the RIVALS Movement and Mechanics Guide.

Katana — The Defensive Masterpiece: Can deflect almost all hitscan weapon bullets. The deflect ability (4s cooldown, 1s duration) is game-changing — timed correctly, it turns an enemy’s attack against them. Essential for gun-melee hybrid playstyles.

Knife — Pure Damage: Fastest reliable TTK among melee weapons. 40 damage at 0.60s cooldown with +10% move speed. The heavy attack (40 damage, 1.25s cooldown, x3.75 crit) rewards patience.

Chainsaw — The Rusher: 60 damage is the highest DPS melee, with a built-in +85% speed boost during its ability. Three-hit kill potential makes it devastating for aggressive rushes.

FAQ#

Q: What’s the best weapon in RIVALS right now? A: Permafrost is the undisputed #1. Its freeze/slow mechanic combined with 2-round burst fire gives it unmatched time-to-kill at mid range. It’s the cornerstone of competitive play.

Q: What’s the best weapon for beginners? A: The Assault Rifle (free, default) is the best starting weapon. It’s reliable at all ranges and forgiving for new players. Once you earn Keys, upgrade to the Burst Rifle (25 Keys) for better accuracy and burst damage.

Q: Is the Paintball Gun better than the Assault Rifle? A: At higher skill levels, yes. The Paintball Gun’s 22 damage and faster fire rate reward good tracking aim. However, the Assault Rifle’s consistency and ammo pool make it more forgiving for newer players.

Q: Which melee weapon should I buy first? A: The Knife (20 Keys) is the best value — fast attack speed, +10% move speed, and the best pure damage TTK. If you prefer aggressive play, the Scythe (~40 Keys) with its dash ability is worth the extra cost.

Q: Is the Katana really worth buying for bullet deflect? A: Absolutely. The Katana’s deflect ability can turn entire fights. A well-timed deflect against an Assault Rifle or Permafrost user can save your life and create an opening for a counter-attack. It’s one of the most skill-expressive weapons in the game.

Q: What’s the best utility weapon? A: Freeze Ray for crowd control — a 3-second freeze enables guaranteed follow-up kills. Molotov for area denial on small maps. Medkit for sustain in long engagements. The best choice depends on your playstyle.

Q: How many Keys do I need to get all weapons? A: Approximately 740 Keys for all standard weapons and 2,025 Keys for all Prime weapons. Contraband weapons (Permafrost, Distortion) cost 700 Keys each.

Q: What changed in the Season 3 (Update 20) patch? A: Almost every weapon received balance changes. Key changes: Assault Rifle damage nerfed (13→12) but fire rate buffed (0.11s→0.10s), Burst Rifle body damage nerfed (20→18), Energy Rifle damage buffed (15→20), Bow changed from hitscan to projectile, and new weapons Spear and Grappler were added.
