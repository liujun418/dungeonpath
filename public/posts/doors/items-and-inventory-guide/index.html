Understanding DOORS Items#

Items in DOORS are the backbone of your survival strategy. From basic illumination to entity-banishing relics, knowing what each item does, where to find it, and when to use it separates casual players from seasoned survivors.

Unlike many games where items are purchased from a central shop, DOORS scatters items across containers, hidden rooms, and Jeff’s Shops located at specific doors. Resource management is critical — you cannot carry everything, and gold is limited.

This guide covers every known item, its location, cost, and optimal usage strategy. For entity-specific item recommendations, see our DOORS Entities Bestiary.

Before discussing items, understand the currencies you use to acquire them:

Gold is the most important in-run currency. It does not persist between runs, so spend it wisely during each session.

ITEM FAQ#

Q: What is the most important item to buy first? A: The Lighter (100g) is the best first purchase. Dark rooms and Screech are immediate threats, and 100g is easy to accumulate early in a run.

Q: Should I save gold or spend it immediately? A: Spend on essential items immediately (Lighter, Bandages). Save for the Crucifix if you are accumulating gold and already have essentials.

Q: Can I carry multiple of the same item? A: Yes, you can carry multiple copies of most items, but it is generally inefficient. One good light source is better than two cheap ones.

Q: What happens to my items when I die? A: All in-run items and gold are lost when you die. Only permanent currencies (Knobs, Stardust) and out-of-run upgrades persist.

Q: Is the Skeleton Key worth 300g? A: Yes, if you can afford it. Using a Skeleton Key on a locked door guarantees Jeff’s Shop spawns, which can be run-changing. It also opens Skull Locks.

Q: How many Alarm Clocks do I need for The Nest? A: Carry at least 2-3 Alarm Clocks to effectively distract Figure during the Door 200 Anchor activation sequence.

Q: Can I use items during entity encounters? A: Yes. You can use Vitamins during a Seek chase, throw an Alarm Clock during a Figure encounter, or activate a Crucifix when an entity is about to hit you.

Q: What is the Barrel of Starlight? A: It is a rare, powerful banishment item found in containers. Use it as an absolute last resort against entities that are about to kill you.

Q: Do items stack in inventory? A: Consumable items like Bandages and Vitamins stack. Unique items like the Crucifix and Skeleton Key take individual inventory slots.
