What Is DOORS Speedrunning?#

DOORS speedrunning is the practice of completing DOORS floors in the shortest possible time. Unlike casual play where exploration, item collection, and achievement hunting take priority, speedrunning focuses on minimizing time spent on every single element – from door transitions to entity encounters to puzzle solving.

The DOORS speedrunning community is active and competitive, with runs tracked across multiple categories and floors. Whether you’re aiming to beat your personal best or compete for community records, this guide covers everything you need to know.

The Most Common Speedrun-Killer#

Panic during entity encounters is the single biggest time-waster for new speedrunners. When Rush triggers or Figure appears, the instinct to rush (pun intended) causes mistakes. The fix is repetition – practice each entity encounter until your response is automatic, not panicked.

FAQ#

Q: What is the fastest way to beat The Hotel? A: The Any% speedrun focuses on sprinting through all doors, minimizing entity hide times, skipping non-essential containers, and solving puzzles at maximum speed. Top runners complete The Hotel in under 10 minutes.

Q: How do I practice for DOORS speedrunning? A: Start by learning all room layouts and entity patterns through casual runs. Then practice individual sections (puzzles, entity encounters) before combining everything into full speed runs. Use a timer tool like LiveSplit to track your splits.

Q: What’s the hardest floor to speedrun? A: The Mines is generally considered the hardest due to generator rooms, the Nest boss fight requiring precise Alarm Clock timing, and the higher entity density. The Outdoors follows closely due to the Groundskeeper’s instant-death mechanic.

Q: Can I speedrun DOORS with friends? A: Multiplayer speedruns are possible but typically slower due to coordination overhead. Most community records are set in solo runs. However, co-op runs can be fun for practice.

Q: What items should I prioritize in a speedrun? A: Hotel: Lighter, Vitamins. Mines: Shears, Alarm Clocks, Crowbar (if found). Outdoors: Lotus Petals, Vitamins for the escape. Skip everything else unless it directly saves time.

Q: How do I deal with bad RNG in speedruns? A: Have backup strategies. If you don’t find a Crowbar, optimize your generator room route. If entity spawns are unfavorable, adjust your hiding strategy. Good speedrunners adapt to any situation.

Q: Where are DOORS speedrun records tracked? A: Community records are tracked on speedrun.com under the DOORS category, in the official DOORS Discord server, and through YouTube video submissions. Times are self-reported but community-verified.

Q: What’s the difference between Any% and 100% speedruns? A: Any% focuses on reaching the final door by any means, skipping all optional content. 100% requires finding all secrets, encountering all entities, and completing all objectives. 100% runs are significantly longer.

Q: Is the No Damage category viable? A: Yes, but it’s much slower than Any%. You need to prioritize safety over speed, which means longer hiding times, more cautious navigation, and avoiding all risky skips. Top No Damage Hotel runs are around 14 minutes.

Q: What’s the best way to improve my split times? A: Focus on your weakest section first. Use a timer to identify which doors take you the longest, then practice that specific section repeatedly. Small improvements across many sections add up to major total time savings.
