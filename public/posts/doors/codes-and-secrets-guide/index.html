Overview#

DOORS by LSPLASH and Redibles is one of the most content-rich horror games on Roblox, packed with codes, secret rooms, easter eggs, hidden achievements, and developer references that many players never discover. Whether you’re trying to maximize your free Knobs through codes, find every secret room, or uncover the hidden lore scattered throughout The Hotel and The Mines, this guide has everything you need.

Unlike many Roblox games, DOORS rewards thorough exploration and attention to detail. The developers have hidden dozens of secrets across multiple floors, and the community continues to discover new ones years after the game’s launch. For a complete walkthrough of every floor, see our DOORS Beginner Guide.

Active Codes in DOORS (May 2026)#

DOORS codes are promotional strings that players redeem for free Knobs (the permanent out-of-run currency), Stardust, and occasionally special items like Revivals. Knobs are used in the Lobby Shop to buy permanent upgrades and cosmetics.

How to Redeem Codes#

StepAction
1Launch DOORS on Roblox and enter the Lobby
2Walk to or look for the Shop area in the Lobby
3Find the text input box (code redemption field) in the Shop menu
4Type the code exactly as shown — codes are case-sensitive
5Click Redeem or press Enter
6A confirmation message will appear showing your reward (Knobs, Stardust, etc.)

FAQ#

Q: How do I redeem codes in DOORS? A: Open the Shop in the Lobby, find the text input box, type the code exactly as shown (case-sensitive), and click Redeem. The most valuable code is 777, which gives 777 Knobs + Stardust + 1 Revival.

Q: Do DOORS codes expire? A: Yes, most codes expire after a period of time. Some codes like 777 and LAZYDEVS have remained active for extended periods, but seasonal and event codes typically expire within weeks or months.

Q: How many secret rooms are there in DOORS? A: At least 10 secret rooms have been confirmed across all floors: The Library, The Electrical Room, Green House, Painting Room, The Adit, Developer Room, Jeff’s Secret Back Room, The Infirmary, Hidden Courtyard, and Vent System. More may exist.

Q: How do I find the Developer Room? A: The Developer Room is in The Mines floor. Search office and industrial areas for hidden switches or interactive panels disguised as normal wall decorations. It’s one of the hardest secrets to find.

Q: What’s the Skull Lock and how do I open it? A: The Skull Lock is found in The Infirmary (a hidden room in The Hotel). It requires a Skeleton Key (300g from Jeff’s Shop) to open. Inside, you’ll find a Green Herb and potentially other rare items.

Q: How many achievements does DOORS have? A: DOORS has 87+ badges/achievements as of the latest update. This number increases with each major update.

Q: What’s the best code to redeem first? A: 777 — it gives 777 Knobs, Stardust, and a Revival, making it by far the most valuable single code. Redeem it before any other code.

Q: Can I access secret rooms in multiplayer? A: Yes. Secret rooms are accessible in both solo and multiplayer runs. In fact, having more players increases the chances of someone spotting a hidden entrance.

Q: Are there hidden achievements in DOORS? A: Yes. Many achievements are hidden — their names and requirements are not shown until unlocked. Examples include “Secret Seeker” (find all secret rooms), “A Bone To Pick” (open Skull Lock), and “Picking Flowers” (have Grampy create a Lotus Flower).

Q: What is The Archives Update? A: The Archives Update is a major upcoming update (expected May/June 2026) that will revamp The Rooms subfloor, add new entities, re-add vent rooms, improve replayability, and likely include new codes, achievements, and secrets.

Q: How do I unlock The Outdoors? A: You need 10 Stardust, which you can get from the 777 code or gameplay. Once you have Stardust, select The Outdoors from the Lobby to access the garden-themed subfloor.

Q: Is there a speedrun community for DOORS? A: Yes. The DOORS community has active speedrunners who compete in categories like Any% Hotel, Any% Mines, and 100% runs. Check the DOORS Discord and Reddit for speedrun leaderboards and strategies.
