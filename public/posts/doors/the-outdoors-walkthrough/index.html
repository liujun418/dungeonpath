What Is The Outdoors?#

The Outdoors is the third floor of DOORS, representing a dramatic departure from the enclosed hotel corridors and mine tunnels that define the earlier floors. Instead of claustrophobic indoor spaces, The Outdoors places you in a sprawling, garden-themed environment with open pathways, hedge mazes, monuments, and natural hazards.

Despite its serene visual design, The Outdoors is arguably the most mechanically complex floor in DOORS. The entities here are more numerous, the environmental hazards are unforgiving, and the final boss fight demands mastery of every skill you’ve learned in Floors 1 and 2.

How to Access The Outdoors#

MethodRequirement

Stardust is earned through completing runs, achievements, and special events. The 10 Stardust cost is a one-time unlock – once purchased, The Outdoors is permanently available.

Door-by-Door Walkthrough#

Early Outdoors (First Doors): The Garden Entrance#

The opening section of The Outdoors establishes the garden theme and introduces the floor’s core mechanic: environmental awareness.

What to expect:

  1. You emerge from an underground exit into a lush garden area
  2. The first doors lead through manicured paths and flower beds
  3. Groundskeeper appears early and establishes the “don’t step on grass” rule
  4. Standard entities (Rush, Screech) appear to ease you in

1-10Groundskeeper introduction, Rush, ScreechLearn grass patterns; stay on paths
11-20Mandrake encounters, CawsLearn Mandrake retrieval; feed bread to Caws
21-30Monument puzzles beginStudy monument patterns; interact carefully

The Groundskeeper: Core Mechanic Introduction#

The Groundskeeper is the defining entity of The Outdoors and introduces the floor’s most important rule:

DO NOT STEP ON THE GRASS when the Groundskeeper can see you.

AttributeDetail
WarningGroundskeeper points toward monuments or your position
LocationThroughout the garden areas
AttackKills you instantly if it sees you walking on grass
SurvivalStay on designated paths when Groundskeeper is active
Danger Level★★★★★

The Groundskeeper changes how you navigate the entire floor. Every movement decision must account for:

  • Where the Groundskeeper is looking
  • Whether your current position is on grass or a path
  • The safest route to the next door

Strategy: When you see the Groundskeeper, freeze immediately and check your footing. If you’re on a path, stay there. If you’re on grass, wait for the Groundskeeper to look away, then carefully step back onto the path.

Mandrake#

AttributeDetail
WarningScreaming plant appears on paths
AttackContinuous scream drains HP and can kill
SurvivalPick up the Mandrake and return it to its hole before the scream kills you
Danger Level★★★★

Mandrake is a time-pressured puzzle entity. When it appears, it begins screaming immediately. You must:

  1. Approach the Mandrake quickly
  2. Pick it up (interact prompt)
  3. Find its hole (usually nearby, glowing)
  4. Place it back before your HP reaches zero

Pro tip: The hole is always within a short radius of where the Mandrake spawns. Don’t run too far – check the immediate area first.

Monuments are puzzle entities. They present a pattern or sequence that you must replicate. Interacting with the monument out of sequence triggers a possession effect that damages you and disorients your controls.

Monument Puzzle TypeHow to Solve

Surge#

AttributeDetail
WarningElectrical crackling sound; visual distortion
AttackLightning bolt that damages players in the open
SurvivalHide behind monuments or structures when Surge strikes
Danger Level★★★★

Surge is an area-denial entity. When it activates, lightning strikes open areas. You need to find cover behind monuments, structures, or other objects. The key is to recognize the warning signs early and reach cover before the strike.

The Hedge Maze Section (Late Outdoors)#

One of the most distinctive features of The Outdoors is the Hedge Maze, which appears in the late section of the floor.

Hedge Maze FeatureDetail
EntranceMarked by a stone archway
LayoutMultiple dead ends with one correct path
HazardsWorld Lotus vines block the exit
Boss LocationThe center contains the World Lotus final boss

Navigation strategy:

  1. Use the right-hand rule – keep your right hand on the hedge wall at all times and follow it
  2. Mark dead ends mentally – so you don’t repeat mistakes on future attempts
  3. Watch for visual cues – the correct path sometimes has subtle markers (different colored leaves, light patterns)
  4. Stay on paths – the Groundskeeper may still be active in the maze

Final Doors and World Lotus (Boss Fight)#

The final confrontation with the World Lotus takes place at the center of the Hedge Maze.

How the boss fight works:

  1. You reach the center of the Hedge Maze
  2. The World Lotus activates, covering the exit with vines
  3. You must collect Lotus Petals (8 total) scattered around the area
  4. Each petal collection triggers a hazard phase
  5. After collecting all petals, you can craft the Lotus Flower to complete the floor
Boss PhaseChallengeStrategy
Phase 1: Petal SearchFind Lotus Petals around the maze centerScope the area quickly; petals glow faintly
Phase 2: Hazard ActivationEntities spawn when you pick up petalsUse Crucifix for protection; stay mobile
Phase 3: Vine BarrierWorld Lotus blocks the exit with vinesCraft Lotus Flower from collected petals to clear
Phase 4: EscapeRun to the exit while entities pursueUse Vitamins for speed; follow the path

Detailed boss strategy:

  1. Before picking up petals, walk the perimeter and note all petal locations
  2. Plan your collection order – start with the closest petal, work outward
  3. Use a Crucifix if entities swarm you during petal collection
  4. Save Vitamins for Phase 4 – the escape chase
  5. Don’t step on grass – even during the boss fight, the Groundskeeper may be watching

FAQ#

Q: How do I unlock The Outdoors? A: Spend 10 Stardust in the Lobby to unlock The Outdoors. Stardust is earned through completing runs, achievements, and special events.

Q: What is the Groundskeeper and how do I survive it? A: The Groundskeeper kills you instantly if it sees you walking on grass. Stay on designated stone/dirt paths at all times and freeze when the Groundskeeper is looking in your direction.

Q: How do I complete The Outdoors? A: Collect 8 Lotus Petals scattered throughout the floor, craft a Lotus Flower, and use it to clear the World Lotus vines blocking the exit. Then escape to the final door.

Q: What do I do with Mandrake? A: Pick up the Mandrake and return it to its glowing hole before the scream drains all your HP. The hole is always nearby.

Q: How do I solve Monument puzzles? A: Monuments display a pattern or sequence that you must replicate. Watch the displayed pattern carefully, then interact with the monument in the correct sequence.

Q: What is the World Lotus? A: The World Lotus is the final boss of The Outdoors, located at the center of the Hedge Maze. It spawns vines and entities during its boss phases and must be defeated by collecting Lotus Petals and crafting a Lotus Flower.

Q: Can I skip The Outdoors? A: No, if you want to complete the full DOORS storyline, you need to complete The Outdoors. However, you can access other floors (The Hotel, The Mines) independently from the Lobby.

Q: How many achievements are in The Outdoors? A: There are 8+ Outdoors-exclusive achievements, including Touched Grass, Down The Water Spout, Do’h!, A Bone To Pick, Feed The Birds, Picking Flowers, Detour, and Rebirth.

Q: Is The Outdoors harder than The Mines? A: It depends on playstyle. The Outdoors has more environmental awareness requirements (grass, monuments, surge) while The Mines has more intense pressure-based encounters (Figure hunting by sound). Many players find The Outdoors more punishing due to the instant-death grass mechanic.

Q: What’s the best route through The Outdoors? A: The optimal route minimizes time on open paths, uses the right-hand rule in the Hedge Maze, collects Lotus Petals in a systematic order, and saves Vitamins for the final escape. Practice the route to learn entity spawn patterns and optimal movement paths.
