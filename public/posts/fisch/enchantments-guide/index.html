How Enchantments Work in Fisch#

Enchantments are permanent stat boosts applied to your fishing rod using an Enchant Relic at the Anvil NPC. The Anvil is located in the blacksmith area at Moosewood, the starting island. You can find it next to the weapon rack, identifiable by the anvil icon on the minimap.

The Enchantment Process#

  1. Obtain an Enchant Relic — these are rare items that can be fished up or purchased
  2. Travel to Moosewood — the Anvil NPC is at the blacksmith area behind Pierre
  3. Approach the Anvil and interact with it while holding your rod
  4. Select the Enchant Relic from your inventory to consume it
  5. Your rod receives a random enchantment — the result is random from the pool of available enchantments
  6. Check your rod’s new stats in the inventory menu to confirm the enchantment applied

Critical Rule: Each rod can hold only ONE enchantment at a time. Applying a new enchantment completely replaces the existing one. There is no way to remove an enchantment without overwriting it, so think carefully before re-rolling a rare rod.

Every Enchantment Analyzed#

There are 8 enchantments currently available in Fisch. Each one modifies a specific rod stat or adds a unique proc effect. Below is every enchantment with exact numbers, use cases, and tier ranking.

S-Tier Enchantments#

Divine#

The best money-making enchantment in Fisch.

StatEffect
Proc Chance10%
EffectFish sells at 5x value
Best RodsMythic Rod, Ethereal Prism Rod, Masterline Rod
TierS+ (Money)

Divine triggers on approximately 1 in 10 catches. When it procs on a high-value fish, you effectively earn 5 catches worth of coins from a single fish. On the Mythic Rod with its base 60% mutation rate, a Mythic-mutated fish worth 10,000 C$ becomes a 50,000 C$ payday when Divine procs.

The Math: If you average 1,000 C$ per fish and catch one fish every 30 seconds with Divine, you earn:

  • Without Divine: 2,000 C$ per minute
  • With Divine (10% proc): 2,000 C$ + (1,000 x 5 x 0.1) x 2 fish/min = 3,000 C$ effective per minute
  • That is a 50% effective income increase over no enchantment

For maximum income, pair Divine with the best money-making strategies from the Fisch Money Farming Guide.

Lucky#

The best enchantment for catching rare fish and increasing overall catch quality.

StatEffect
Luck Boost+45
EffectIncreases chance of rare fish + mutations
Best RodsDestiny Rod, Hades’ Soul-Scythe, No-Life Rod
TierS (Rare Farming)

Lucky gives a flat +45 to your rod’s luck stat. This compounds with your rod’s base luck to significantly increase your chances of hooking rare fish and rolling mutations. On a rod like the Destiny Rod (base 180% luck), Lucky pushes luck to 225%, which puts you in the top tier for exotic fish spawns.

Why Lucky Can Be Better Than Mutated: While Mutated boosts mutation rate specifically, Lucky improves your entire catch table. You get more rare fish AND more mutations. The broad improvement makes Lucky the better all-rounder.

Hasty#

Maximizes fish-per-hour by reducing reel time.

StatEffect
Reel Speed Boost+55
EffectFish reel in much faster
Best RodsFast Castbound, No-Life Rod
TierS (Speed)

Hasty reduces the time spent in the reeling minigame by up to 50% depending on the fish. For volume-based farming strategies (catching many fish quickly rather than targeting specific rare fish), Hasty is unmatched. Combined with a rod that already has high lure speed like the Masterline Rod (1000% lure), Hasty creates the fastest catch cycle possible.

A-Tier Enchantments#

Mutated#

Specialized enchantment for mutation hunters.

StatEffect
Mutation Rate Boost+15%
EffectFish spawn with mutations more often
Best RodsTrident Rod, Mythic Rod
TierA (Mutation Hunting)

Mutated adds 15 percentage points to your mutation rate. This is particularly effective on the Trident Rod, which already has high mutation chance as a base stat. The Mutated enchantment stacks multiplicatively with luck boosts from bait, making it a strong choice for dedicated mutation farmers.

Mutation Rate Formula: Final Mutation Chance = Base Rod Mutation Rate + Mutated Bonus + (Luck Stat x 0.05)

Example with Trident Rod (base 30% mutation rate):

  • Trident Rod alone: 30%
  • Trident Rod + Mutated: 45%
  • Trident Rod + Mutated + 150% luck: 45% + 7.5% = 52.5%

Swift#

Best for players who hate waiting for bites.

StatEffect
Lure Speed Boost+55
EffectFish bite significantly faster
Best RodsAny rod with low base lure speed
TierA (Quality of Life)

Swift reduces the time between casts by increasing how quickly fish approach your bait. On rods with low base lure speed (under 50%), Swift is transformative — it cuts wait time roughly in half. However, on rods that already have 99%+ lure speed (like Masterline or Castbound), Swift provides no additional benefit since lure speed is capped at 99%.

B-Tier Enchantments#

Magnetic#

Solid early-game enchantment for faster fishing.

StatEffect

Best RodsEarly-game rods (Carbon, Steady, Plastic)
TierB (Early Game)

Magnetic increases how frequently fish bite your line. While this sounds similar to Swift, Magnetic affects bite frequency (how often fish spawn near your bait) rather than lure speed (how fast they swim to it). The net effect is similar — more fish per hour — but Magnetic makes more of a difference when fishing in spots with low fish density.

For beginners using basic rods, Magnetic is often the best choice because rod stats are so low that any improvement has outsized impact.

Resilient reduces the rate at which your line’s durability drains during the reeling minigame. This gives you more time to land difficult fish without them breaking the line. It is strictly a quality-of-life enchantment — it does not increase your catch value or quantity, but it does reduce frustration.

Worth noting: The recent Fisch updates have reduced durability loss across all rods, making Unbreakable less valuable than it was in 2025. Unless you specifically AFK fish for 8+ hours at a time, choose a different enchantment.

Fish Mutations: Complete Reference#

Mutations are special visual effects and value multipliers that can appear on any fish you catch. They are one of the most important mechanics in Fisch because a single good mutation can multiply a fish’s value dramatically.

How Mutations Work#

When you catch a fish, the game rolls for mutations sequentially. Each mutation has a base chance that is modified by your rod’s luck stat, active bait bonuses, and any enchantments (like Mutated). Multiple mutations can stack on a single fish, though the chances of rolling 2+ rare mutations simultaneously are very low.

Mutation Roll Order:

  1. Game determines if fish catches (based on luck + rod stats)
  2. Game rolls for common mutations first (Albino, Silver, etc.)
  3. Game rolls for rare mutations (Mythic, Abyssal, etc.)
  4. Game rolls for conditional mutations (Sunken, Frozen, Electric — location-based)
  5. All multipliers are applied multiplicatively to calculate final value

Best Enchantment + Rod Combos#

For Money Farming: Mythic Rod + Divine#

The undisputed king of coin generation. The Mythic Rod’s base 60% mutation rate ensures almost every fish has some mutation bonus. When Divine procs, it multiplies an already mutated fish by 5x.

Expected output: 150,000-300,000 C$ per hour at Grand Reef or Volcanic Vents.

Setup:

  • Rod: Mythic Rod
  • Enchantment: Divine
  • Bait: Seaweed (for luck) or Worm (for universal attraction)
  • Location: Grand Reef (best mix of rare + common fish)
  • Technique: Focus on quantity — Divine is a numbers game

For Mutation Hunting: Trident Rod + Mutated#

The Trident Rod’s base 30% mutation rate plus Mutated’s +15% pushes you to 45% minimum. With good luck boosts, you can reach 55%+ mutation rate.

Expected output: 40-60% of all catches will have at least one mutation.

Setup:

  • Rod: Trident Rod
  • Enchantment: Mutated
  • Bait: Coral (luck boost) or Shrimp (universal)
  • Location: Roslit Bay or Depths (high rare fish concentration)
  • Technique: Keep every fish that has 2+ mutations; sell the rest

For Rare Fish: Destiny Rod + Lucky#

The Destiny Rod’s built-in luck boost combined with Lucky enchantment gives the highest total luck stat of any non-endgame rod combination.

Expected output: Significantly more exotic and legendary-grade fish per hour compared to any other mid-game setup.

Setup:

  • Rod: Destiny Rod
  • Enchantment: Lucky
  • Bait: Fish Head (rare fish bait) or Truffle Worm (high luck)
  • Location: Grand Reef or Depths
  • Technique: Target specific rare fish locations; use radar accessories

For Endgame: Masterline Rod + Hasty#

Once you have the Masterline Rod, its infinite weight capacity and 1000% lure speed already give you perfect utility. Hasty maximizes the catch-and-release cycle speed for the fastest farming possible.

Expected output: 200,000-400,000 C$ per hour at any location.

Setup:

  • Rod: Masterline Rod
  • Enchantment: Hasty
  • Bait: Quality Bait (any high-tier bait for bonus luck)
  • Location: Any high-density fishing spot
  • Technique: Rapid cast, reel, appraise, repeat cycle

For AFK Farming: Any Rod + Unbreakable#

For overnight AFK sessions, Unbreakable is a requirement. Without it, your rod will break within 2-3 hours of continuous use.

Setup:

  • Rod: Any rod with good base stats (No-Life Rod recommended for free option)
  • Enchantment: Unbreakable
  • Bait: Any cheap bait unlimited supply
  • Location: Moosewood dock (safe, no aggressive fish)
  • Duration: Up to 12 hours without rod failure

Common Enchantment Mistakes to Avoid#

Mistake 1: Enchanting early-game rods with rare relics. Do not waste Enchant Relics on rods you plan to replace within days. The Plastic Rod, Carbon Rod, and even the Steady Rod are temporary tools. Save your relics for rods you will use long-term, like the Mythic Rod, Trident Rod, or Destiny Rod.

Mistake 2: Chasing the perfect enchantment. Each enchantment application is random. Players have spent 20+ relics trying to get Divine on a specific rod. If you get Lucky or Hasty on your first try, consider keeping it — both are excellent general-purpose enchantments, and the marginal gain from re-rolling is not worth the time investment.

Mistake 3: Ignoring the Anvil after rod upgrades. When you purchase a new rod, it comes with no enchantment. Always budget for an Enchant Relic whenever you upgrade rods. A rod without an enchantment is performing at only 70-80% of its potential.

Mistake 4: Unbreakable on active-play rods. Unless you specifically AFK fish for hours at a time, Unbreakable is the weakest enchantment. Repairing your rod costs a tiny fraction of what you earn from fishing. Never take Unbreakable on a rod you actively use.

Advanced Enchantment Strategy#

The Relic Economy#

Enchant Relics have a stable player-to-player trading value of approximately 5,000-15,000 C$ depending on supply. During events, relic prices can spike to 20,000+ C$ as demand increases. If you need coins quickly, consider selling excess relics rather than using them.

When to Re-Roll#

Re-rolling (applying a new enchantment over an existing one) is expensive but sometimes necessary. Here is when it makes sense:

Enchantment Progression Path#

As you progress through Fisch, your enchantment needs change:

  1. Early Game (Plastic Rod to Steady Rod): Magnetic or Swift — speed up the grind to afford better rods. Do not use rare relics here; only common-tier relics if you have spares.

  2. Mid Game (Mythic Rod to Destiny Rod): Divine (for money) or Lucky (for rare hunting). This is where enchantments matter most because you are spending significant time with these rods.

  3. Late Game (Trident Rod, Hades’ Soul-Scythe): Divine or Mutated — maximize your per-fish value as you target specific mutations.

  4. End Game (Masterline Rod, Castbound): Hasty or Lucky — squeeze every drop of efficiency from the best rods in the game.

Frequently Asked Questions#

Q: Can I have two enchantments on one rod? A: No. Each rod can hold exactly one enchantment at a time. Applying a new enchantment overwrites the existing one.

Q: Does the enchantment stay if I dye my rod? A: Yes. Enchantments are tied to the rod, not its appearance. Dyeing, renaming, or adding accessories does not affect enchantments.

Q: Can I transfer an enchantment to a new rod? A: No. Enchantments are consumed when applied. You must use a new Enchant Relic on each rod you want to enchant.

Q: Do enchantments work on all rods? A: Yes, every rod can receive enchantments. However, some rods have stat caps that make certain enchantments wasted (e.g., Swift on a rod already at 99% lure speed).

Q: Is there a way to guarantee a specific enchantment? A: No. Enchantment results are completely random. There are no methods, tricks, or glitches to influence which enchantment you receive.

Q: Does the Anvil cost coins to use? A: Using the Anvil itself is free. The only cost is the Enchant Relic you consume in the process.
