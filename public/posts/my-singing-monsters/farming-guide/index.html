Coin Farming#

Coins are the most important early-game resource and remain relevant throughout your MSM journey.

How Coin Production Works#

Every monster produces coins based on its level, happiness, and base coin rate. Coins accumulate in the monster’s “bank” and can be collected by tapping the monster.

FactorEffect on Coin Production
Monster LevelHigher level = more coins per minute (see table below)
Monster RarityEpic > Rare > Common
Monster Element Count4-element > 3-element > 2-element > 1-element (generally)
Happiness25% bonus when all monsters on island are 100% happy
Wishing Torch boostNo direct coin boost, but more monsters = more coins
Island structureEach island generates coins independently

Treat Farming Tips#

  1. Upgrade Bakeries early – Even one upgrade dramatically improves treat output
  2. Run Bakeries continuously – Always have treats cooking, even if you don’t need them yet
  3. Switch to Candy at mid-game – Better treats-per-coin ratio than Cookies
  4. Cake for high-level pushes – Only use Cake when pushing monsters past Level 15
  5. Wublin treats supplement – Screemu and Bona-Petite provide free treats daily

Conclusion#

Resource farming in My Singing Monsters follows a clear progression: coins first, then diamonds, then shards, with treats as the bottleneck throughout. The early game is about establishing coin income through Entbrats and 3-element monsters. The mid game shifts to Wublin Island for passive diamond income. The endgame optimizes every island for maximum resource production.

Remember these key principles:

  1. 4-element monsters are the most efficient coin producers per bed
  2. Wublin Island is the best diamond source for free-to-play players
  3. Treats are the real bottleneck – upgrade Bakeries early and run them constantly
  4. Level monsters evenly before pushing individual monsters very high
  5. Collect frequently – idle monsters stop producing when their bank is full
