Understanding Palworld’s Biome System#

The Palpagos Islands are divided into distinct biomes, each hosting a unique ecosystem of Pals. Your survival gear determines which biomes you can safely explore – you need heat-resistant armor for the Volcano and cold-resistant gear for the Snow/Ice Mountain.

Paladius and Necromus Location#

Paladius and Necromus spawn together on the Desert Plateau in the far northeast of the map. They are the only legendary Pals that appear as a duo. You must defeat both. Recommended level: 45+. Each drops legendary schematics and Ancient Civilization Parts.

Frostallion Location#

Frostallion roams the peak of the Frostbound Mountains in the far north. It is level 50, drops legendary schematics, and is widely considered one of the best Pals in the game for both combat and as a flying mount. Its Frostallion saddle unlocks at level 46.

Pro Tips for Pal Hunting#

  1. Sphere economy: Use basic Spheres for common Pals (level 1-20), Mega Spheres for levels 20-35, Giga Spheres for 35-45, and Hyper Spheres / Legendary Spheres for 45-50+. See the Resources & Materials Guide for sphere crafting requirements.

  2. Capture power upgrades: Upgrade your capture power at the Statue of Power using Lifmunk Effigies. Maxed capture power makes high-level catches significantly easier.

  3. Back shots matter: Hitting a Pal from behind with a Pal Sphere increases capture chance by approximately 10%.

  4. Status effects help: Poison, burn, and sleep all increase capture rate modifier when combined with low HP.

  5. Use the right sphere type: A Mega Sphere on a level 30 Pal is waste. Always match sphere tier to Pal level.

  6. Catch 10 of everything: The Statue of Power gives permanent stat bonuses for catching 10 of each Pal species.

  7. Rare Pals have set spawns: Legendary Pals (Frostallion, Jetragon, Paladius, Necromus) always spawn in the same location after being defeated or captured.

  8. Paldeck completion bonus: Completing entries rewards bonus XP and permanent stat increases. Prioritize catching new species.
