What Is Palworld?#

Palworld is an open-world survival crafting game developed by Pocketpair, released into Early Access on January 19, 2024. Often described as “Pokemon with guns,” Palworld blends monster taming, base building, automation, combat, and exploration into a single experience. As of 2026, the game has received major content updates including the Sakurajima update and the Feybreak update, expanding the map, adding new Pals, and refining the endgame.

You play as a mysterious amnesiac stranded on the Palpagos Islands. Your goal: survive, build, capture Pals, and uncover the secrets of the islands. What sets Palworld apart from other survival games is its automation layer – your captured Pals can work in your base, crafting items, farming resources, and defending against raids while you explore.

First Steps: Spawn and Immediate Survival#

1. Spawn Location#

You start on a beach in the Grasslands region near the Sea Breeze Archipelago. The first few minutes are about gathering basic resources by hand. Here is your immediate priority list:

PriorityTaskWhy
1Punch trees for WoodBasic building material
2Collect Stone from the groundTools and building foundations
3Gather Fibers from bushesCloth, torches, tools
4Pick up Berries from bushesFood – your hunger depletes fast
5Craft a Stone Pickaxe and Stone AxeFaster resource gathering
6Craft a TorchNight vision and warmth
7Catch your first PalAutomates gathering, aids combat

2. Hunger and Stamina Systems#

Palworld has two critical player stats: Hunger and Stamina.

StatEffect When DepletedHow to Recover
Hunger (Food Bar)Health stops regenerating; you take damage over timeEat Berries, Cooked Meat, or any food item
HP (Health)Reduced to 0 = death, respawn with item lossSleep in bed, consume healing items
Stamina (Green Bar)Cannot sprint, climb, dodge, or attackStop moving briefly; stamina regenerates automatically

Your hunger bar depletes continuously. In the early game, Berries are your primary food source. Cooked Berries (craft at a campfire) restore more hunger than raw berries. Always carry a stack of food.

Pro tip: Raw food spoils in your inventory over time. Cooked food lasts longer. Once you build a Cooler Box (level 6 technology), food spoils much slower.

Catching Your First Pals#

To catch Pals, you need Pal Spheres, which unlock at level 2 in the Technology tree. Each Pal Sphere requires:

  • 3x Wood
  • 3x Stone
  • 1x Paldium Fragment

Paldium Fragments are found on the ground near rocks – they glow blue. You will also mine them from small blue ore deposits.

The Capture Mechanic#

Weaken a Pal by reducing its HP (lower HP = higher capture chance). Then throw a Pal Sphere. A mini-game plays: a circle shrinks and expands. Press the button when the circle is at its smallest for a critical capture bonus.

For a complete map of every Pal’s habitat, check the Pal Locations Guide.

Building Your First Base#

Choosing a Location#

Your first base location matters immensely (see our Base Building Guide for detailed location analysis and layouts). Look for a flat area with:

  • Access to trees and stone (for building materials)
  • Nearby ore deposits (for early metal)
  • Water access (some recipes require water)
  • Open space (at least 10x10 foundation area)

The area near the Sea Breeze Archipelago starting beach is excellent for a first base. It has abundant resources, flat terrain, and manageable raid difficulty.

Base Building Basics#

You unlock the Palbox at level 2. This is the heart of your base. Place it on flat ground – the blue circle it creates defines your base boundary.

Pal Assignment#

Each Pal in your base will auto-assign to tasks based on its Work Suitability stats. You can manually assign Pals by picking them up and throwing them at a workstation.

Keep your base population balanced. You will want at least one Planter, one Waterer, and one Gatherer for a self-sustaining food system.

Leveling Up: Experience and Technology#

How XP Works#

You gain experience from:

ActivityXP GainedNotes
Catching a new PalHigh (scales by Pal level)Best XP source – catch 10 of each Pal type
Defeating PalsLow-ModerateScales by Pal level relative to yours
Crafting itemsLowPassive gain while working at base
Exploring new areasLowDiscover fast travel points
Defeating Tower BossesVery HighOne-time per boss

Best leveling strategy: Catch 10 of every Pal species you encounter. The bonus XP for the 10th catch is significant, and you get bonus stats from the Statue of Power for each Pal you catch.

Early Game Priorities Checklist#

Here is a level-by-level roadmap from spawn to level 25:

Levels 1-5 (Hour 1-2):

  • Gather wood, stone, fiber by hand
  • Catch 1 Lamball, 1 Cattiva, 1 Foxsparks
  • Build Palbox and wooden shelter (4 walls, foundation, roof, door)
  • Build a bed and campfire
  • Cook berries and catch 10 of each Pal species encountered
  • Build Berry Plantation

Levels 6-10 (Hour 2-4):

  • Craft Cloth Outfit for armor
  • Unlock and build Cooler Box
  • Catch a Nitewing (flying mount) or a Rushoar (ground mount)
  • Explore the first tower dungeon (Rayne Syndicate – Zoe & Grizzbolt, recommended level 11+)
  • Build a Furnace and start smelting ore
  • Collect Paldium for Pal Spheres

Levels 11-15 (Hour 4-6):

  • Unlock the Ranch and assign Lamball for wool
  • Craft better armor (Pelt Armor)
  • Build a second base near ore deposits
  • Explore and capture Fire and Electric Pals
  • Build the Statue of Power and start upgrading

Levels 16-20 (Hour 6-10):

  • Unlock the Crossbow
  • Explore the desert and volcanic biomes (prepare heat-resistant armor)
  • Capture a Digtoise for fast ore mining
  • Build the Egg Incubator and start hatching eggs

Feeding Your Pals#

Pals eat from the Feed Box in your base. They auto-feed when hungry. Higher-quality food keeps Sanity high.

Food TypeBest ForSanity Restoration
Raw BerriesEmergency only (low Sanity gain)Minimal
Cooked BerriesEarly game default+1
Baked BerriesBetter early option+2
SaladMid-game base food (level 36)+15
PizzaEndgame base food+20

Optimal base food progression: Raw berries (level 5) -> Cooked berries (level 5 + campfire) -> Baked berries (level 11+ with Cooking Pot) -> Salad (level 36) -> Pizza (level 40+).
