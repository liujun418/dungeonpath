## 3. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

### 3.1 What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites you visit. They are widely used to make websites function efficiently and to provide information to the site operators. Cookies may be "persistent" (remain on your device until they expire or are deleted) or "session" (deleted when you close your browser).

### 3.2 Cookies Used on This Site

| Cookie Type | Source | Purpose | Duration | Personal Data? |
|------------|--------|---------|----------|----------------|
| **Locale Preference** | First-party (dungeonpath.com) | Store your language/locale choice | Session | No |
| **Google Analytics** | Third-party (Google LLC) | Site analytics, traffic measurement | Up to 2 years | Anonymized |
| **Google AdSense DART Cookie** | Third-party (Google LLC) | Interest-based advertising | Up to 2 years | Yes (browsing behavior) |
| **AdSense Third-Party Cookies** | Third-party (advertisers via Google) | Ad frequency capping, ad measurement | Varies | Pseudonymized |

### 3.3 Google AdSense DART Cookie

The Site displays advertisements served by **Google AdSense**, a service provided by Google LLC ("Google"). Google and its third-party advertising partners use cookies — including the **DART (Dynamic Advertising Reporting and Targeting) cookie** — to serve ads based on your prior visits to our Site and/or other websites on the internet.

- The DART cookie enables Google and its partners to serve interest-based advertisements to you based on your browsing activity across the web.
- Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visit to our Site and/or other sites on the internet.
- You may review Google's Privacy Policy at [https://policies.google.com/privacy](https://policies.google.com/privacy).
- You may view and manage your Google ad personalization settings at [https://www.google.com/settings/ads](https://www.google.com/settings/ads).

### 3.4 Google Analytics (GA4)

We use **Google Analytics 4 (GA4)** to understand how visitors interact with the Site. GA4 collects anonymized usage data including:

- Pages visited and navigation paths
- Time spent on each page
- Approximate geographic location (country/region level)
- Device and browser characteristics
- Referring website or search engine

GA4 does **not** store IP addresses in full — IP anonymization is enabled. GA4 does not use or share personally identifiable information. For more information, see Google's [Analytics Privacy & Security](https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/6004245) documentation.

### 3.5 Managing Cookies

You have full control over cookie preferences:

| Method | How To |
|--------|--------|
| **Browser Settings** | Most browsers allow you to block, delete, or receive alerts for cookies via Settings > Privacy & Security. Disabling cookies may affect site functionality. |
| **Google Ads Settings** | Visit [https://www.google.com/settings/ads](https://www.google.com/settings/ads) to manage Google ad personalization. |
| **Network Advertising Initiative (NAI)** | Opt out of participating third-party ad networks at [https://www.networkadvertising.org/choices/](https://www.networkadvertising.org/choices/). |
| **Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA)** | Opt out at [https://www.aboutads.info/choices/](https://www.aboutads.info/choices/). |
| **Do Not Track** | We honor Do Not Track (DNT) browser signals where technically supported. |
