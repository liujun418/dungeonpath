## What Is The Outdoors?

**The Outdoors** is the third floor of DOORS, representing a dramatic departure from the enclosed hotel corridors and mine tunnels that define the earlier floors. Instead of claustrophobic indoor spaces, The Outdoors places you in a sprawling, garden-themed environment with open pathways, hedge mazes, monuments, and natural hazards.

Despite its serene visual design, The Outdoors is arguably the most mechanically complex floor in DOORS. The entities here are more numerous, the environmental hazards are unforgiving, and the final boss fight demands mastery of every skill you've learned in Floors 1 and 2.

### How to Access The Outdoors

| Method | Requirement |
|--------|------------|
| **From the Lobby** | Spend **10 Stardust** in the Lobby to unlock The Outdoors |
| **Story progression** | Complete the storyline by finding all Lotus Petals and crafting a Lotus Flower |

**Stardust** is earned through completing runs, achievements, and special events. The 10 Stardust cost is a one-time unlock -- once purchased, The Outdoors is permanently available.

## Door-by-Door Walkthrough

### Early Outdoors (First Doors): The Garden Entrance

The opening section of The Outdoors establishes the garden theme and introduces the floor's core mechanic: **environmental awareness**.

**What to expect:**
1. You emerge from an underground exit into a lush garden area
2. The first doors lead through manicured paths and flower beds
3. **Groundskeeper** appears early and establishes the "don't step on grass" rule
4. Standard entities (Rush, Screech) appear to ease you in

| Door Range | Key Encounters | Strategy |
|------------|---------------|----------|
| **1-10** | Groundskeeper introduction, Rush, Screech | Learn grass patterns; stay on paths |
| **11-20** | Mandrake encounters, Caws | Learn Mandrake retrieval; feed bread to Caws |
| **21-30** | Monument puzzles begin | Study monument patterns; interact carefully |

### The Groundskeeper: Core Mechanic Introduction

**The Groundskeeper** is the defining entity of The Outdoors and introduces the floor's most important rule:

**DO NOT STEP ON THE GRASS when the Groundskeeper can see you.**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Warning** | Groundskeeper points toward monuments or your position |
| **Location** | Throughout the garden areas |
| **Attack** | Kills you instantly if it sees you walking on grass |
| **Survival** | **Stay on designated paths** when Groundskeeper is active |
| **Danger Level** | ★★★★★ |

The Groundskeeper changes how you navigate the entire floor. Every movement decision must account for:
- Where the Groundskeeper is looking
- Whether your current position is on grass or a path
- The safest route to the next door

**Strategy:** When you see the Groundskeeper, **freeze immediately** and check your footing. If you're on a path, stay there. If you're on grass, wait for the Groundskeeper to look away, then carefully step back onto the path.

### Mid Outdoors (Doors 31-60): Escalating Threats

The middle section introduces the full roster of Outdoors entities and combines them in ways that test your multitasking ability.

| Door Range | Key Encounters | Strategy |
|------------|---------------|----------|
| **31-40** | Mandrake + Rush combinations | Handle Mandrake first, then deal with Rush |
| **41-50** | Monument puzzles, Surge appearances | Memorize monument patterns; dodge Surge |
| **51-60** | Caws, environmental hazards | Carry bread for Caws achievements; watch terrain |

#### Mandrake

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Warning** | Screaming plant appears on paths |
| **Attack** | Continuous scream drains HP and can kill |
| **Survival** | **Pick up the Mandrake and return it to its hole** before the scream kills you |
| **Danger Level** | ★★★★ |

Mandrake is a time-pressured puzzle entity. When it appears, it begins screaming immediately. You must:
1. **Approach the Mandrake quickly**
2. **Pick it up** (interact prompt)
3. **Find its hole** (usually nearby, glowing)
4. **Place it back** before your HP reaches zero

**Pro tip:** The hole is always within a short radius of where the Mandrake spawns. Don't run too far -- check the immediate area first.

#### Monument

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Warning** | Pillar-like structure activates |
| **Attack** | Can possess or control players who interact incorrectly |
| **Survival** | **Follow the monument's puzzle pattern** correctly; don't touch randomly |
| **Danger Level** | ★★★ |

Monuments are puzzle entities. They present a pattern or sequence that you must replicate. Interacting with the monument out of sequence triggers a possession effect that damages you and disorients your controls.

| Monument Puzzle Type | How to Solve |
|---------------------|--------------|
| **Symbol matching** | Replicate the displayed symbol sequence on the monument |
| **Timed interaction** | Press the monument's interaction point within the time window |
| **Pattern memory** | Watch the sequence, then repeat it from memory |

#### Surge

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Warning** | Electrical crackling sound; visual distortion |
| **Attack** | Lightning bolt that damages players in the open |
| **Survival** | **Hide behind monuments or structures** when Surge strikes |
| **Danger Level** | ★★★★ |

Surge is an area-denial entity. When it activates, lightning strikes open areas. You need to find cover behind monuments, structures, or other objects. The key is to **recognize the warning signs early** and reach cover before the strike.

### The Hedge Maze Section (Late Outdoors)

One of the most distinctive features of The Outdoors is the **Hedge Maze**, which appears in the late section of the floor.

| Hedge Maze Feature | Detail |
|-------------------|--------|
| **Entrance** | Marked by a stone archway |
| **Layout** | Multiple dead ends with one correct path |
| **Hazards** | World Lotus vines block the exit |
| **Boss Location** | The center contains the **World Lotus** final boss |

**Navigation strategy:**
1. **Use the right-hand rule** -- keep your right hand on the hedge wall at all times and follow it
2. **Mark dead ends mentally** -- so you don't repeat mistakes on future attempts
3. **Watch for visual cues** -- the correct path sometimes has subtle markers (different colored leaves, light patterns)
4. **Stay on paths** -- the Groundskeeper may still be active in the maze

### Final Doors and World Lotus (Boss Fight)

The final confrontation with the **World Lotus** takes place at the center of the Hedge Maze.

**How the boss fight works:**
1. You reach the center of the Hedge Maze
2. The World Lotus activates, covering the exit with vines
3. You must **collect Lotus Petals** (8 total) scattered around the area
4. Each petal collection triggers a hazard phase
5. After collecting all petals, you can **craft the Lotus Flower** to complete the floor

| Boss Phase | Challenge | Strategy |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| **Phase 1: Petal Search** | Find Lotus Petals around the maze center | Scope the area quickly; petals glow faintly |
| **Phase 2: Hazard Activation** | Entities spawn when you pick up petals | Use Crucifix for protection; stay mobile |
| **Phase 3: Vine Barrier** | World Lotus blocks the exit with vines | Craft Lotus Flower from collected petals to clear |
| **Phase 4: Escape** | Run to the exit while entities pursue | Use Vitamins for speed; follow the path |

**Detailed boss strategy:**
1. **Before picking up petals**, walk the perimeter and note all petal locations
2. **Plan your collection order** -- start with the closest petal, work outward
3. **Use a Crucifix** if entities swarm you during petal collection
4. **Save Vitamins for Phase 4** -- the escape chase
5. **Don't step on grass** -- even during the boss fight, the Groundskeeper may be watching

## Environmental Hazards

The Outdoors introduces environmental hazards that exist independently of entity encounters:

### Grass (Groundskeeper Territory)

| Hazard | Effect | Avoidance |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Grass on paths** | Instant death if Groundskeeper sees you walking on it | Stay on designated stone/dirt paths |
| **Grass in open areas** | Same kill condition | Never cut across grass patches |
| **Grass near doors** | Most dangerous area -- easy to miss | Check footing before approaching doors |

### Hedge Maze Dead Ends

| Hazard | Effect | Avoidance |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Wrong turns** | Waste time, expose you to entities | Use right-hand rule; mark dead ends |
| **Vine-blocked exits** | Prevents progress until boss is defeated | Collect Lotus Petals and craft Lotus Flower |

### Lightning Zones (Surge Territory)

| Hazard | Effect | Avoidance |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Open sky areas** | Surge lightning strikes | Stay near monuments or structures |
| **Exposed paths** | Vulnerable during Surge events | Wait out Surge behind cover |

### Environmental Hazard Priority

| Hazard | Lethality | Frequency | Mitigation Difficulty |

| Lightning (Surge) | High damage | Periodic | Medium (find cover) |

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock The Outdoors?**
A: Spend **10 Stardust** in the Lobby to unlock The Outdoors. Stardust is earned through completing runs, achievements, and special events.

**Q: What is the Groundskeeper and how do I survive it?**
A: The Groundskeeper kills you instantly if it sees you walking on grass. **Stay on designated stone/dirt paths at all times** and freeze when the Groundskeeper is looking in your direction.

**Q: How do I complete The Outdoors?**
A: Collect **8 Lotus Petals** scattered throughout the floor, craft a **Lotus Flower**, and use it to clear the World Lotus vines blocking the exit. Then escape to the final door.

**Q: What do I do with Mandrake?**
A: Pick up the Mandrake and return it to its glowing hole before the scream drains all your HP. The hole is always nearby.

**Q: How do I solve Monument puzzles?**
A: Monuments display a pattern or sequence that you must replicate. Watch the displayed pattern carefully, then interact with the monument in the correct sequence.

**Q: What is the World Lotus?**
A: The World Lotus is the final boss of The Outdoors, located at the center of the Hedge Maze. It spawns vines and entities during its boss phases and must be defeated by collecting Lotus Petals and crafting a Lotus Flower.

**Q: Can I skip The Outdoors?**
A: No, if you want to complete the full DOORS storyline, you need to complete The Outdoors. However, you can access other floors (The Hotel, The Mines) independently from the Lobby.

**Q: How many achievements are in The Outdoors?**
A: There are **8+ Outdoors-exclusive achievements**, including Touched Grass, Down The Water Spout, Do'h!, A Bone To Pick, Feed The Birds, Picking Flowers, Detour, and Rebirth.

**Q: Is The Outdoors harder than The Mines?**
A: It depends on playstyle. The Outdoors has more **environmental awareness** requirements (grass, monuments, surge) while The Mines has more intense **pressure-based encounters** (Figure hunting by sound). Many players find The Outdoors more punishing due to the instant-death grass mechanic.

**Q: What's the best route through The Outdoors?**
A: The optimal route minimizes time on open paths, uses the right-hand rule in the Hedge Maze, collects Lotus Petals in a systematic order, and saves Vitamins for the final escape. Practice the route to learn entity spawn patterns and optimal movement paths.
