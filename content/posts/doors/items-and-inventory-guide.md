## Understanding DOORS Items

Items in DOORS are the backbone of your survival strategy. From basic illumination to entity-banishing relics, knowing what each item does, where to find it, and when to use it separates casual players from seasoned survivors.

Unlike many games where items are purchased from a central shop, DOORS scatters items across containers, hidden rooms, and Jeff's Shops located at specific doors. Resource management is critical — you cannot carry everything, and gold is limited.

This guide covers every known item, its location, cost, and optimal usage strategy. For entity-specific item recommendations, see our [DOORS Entities Bestiary](/posts/doors/entities-bestiary/).

---

## Currency Overview

Before discussing items, understand the currencies you use to acquire them:

| Currency | Type | How to Earn | How to Spend |
|----------|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Gold** | In-run | Open containers, find on ground, defeat entities | Jeff's Shop items during the run |
| **Knobs** | Permanent | Codes, achievements, daily login | Lobby Shop permanent upgrades |
| **Stardust** | Permanent | Complete The Outdoors, achievements | Unlock The Outdoors (10 Stardust) |
| **Revives** | Consumable | Codes (777), Lobby Shop, achievements | Auto-revive on death |

**Gold** is the most important in-run currency. It does not persist between runs, so spend it wisely during each session.

## THE HOTEL ITEMS

### Jeff's Shop — The Hotel (Doors 52-59)

Jeff's Shop appears between Doors 52 and 59 in The Hotel. Here is every available item. For a complete bestiary covering every entity, see our [DOORS Entities Bestiary](/posts/doors/entities-bestiary/).

#### Core Items

| Item | Price | Description | Priority |
|------|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Lighter** | 100g | Basic light source with limited fuel duration | **High** — Essential for dark rooms and Screech prevention |
| **Bandages** | 100g | Restores a moderate amount of HP | **High** — Always carry at least one |
| **Vitamins** | 100g | Temporary speed boost for a short duration | **Medium** — Situational but useful |

| Tip | Details |

| **Skeleton Key guarantees a shop** | If your Skeleton Key opens a locked door, Jeff's Shop is guaranteed to spawn |
| **Don't buy Bandages if you are at full HP** | Save gold for essential items; buy healing when damaged |
| **Vitamins for Seek chase** | Use Vitamins right before a Seek encounter for a speed advantage |

## ITEM USAGE STRATEGIES

### Light Source Priority

The single most important item category. Without light, you are vulnerable to Screech, Gloombats, and general disorientation.

| Situation | Recommended Item | Reason |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| **Early Hotel (Doors 1-50)** | Lighter (100g) | Cheap and sufficient for early dark rooms |
| **Mid Hotel (Doors 50-100)** | Flashlight (200g) | Longer duration for more dangerous areas |
| **The Mines** | Bulklight (300g) | Longest battery for the darkest areas |
| **Gloombat-heavy rooms** | Glowstick | AoE light repels entire swarms |
| **Figure encounters** | No light (turn off) | Light can reveal your position to Figure |
| **The Nest (Door 200)** | No light | Figure hunts by sound; light is irrelevant |

### When to Use Each Item

| Item | When to Use | When NOT to Use |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Bandages** | When HP drops below 50% | When you are at full HP or above 80% |
| **Vitamins** | Right before Seek chases or Figure encounters | In safe rooms (wasted) |
| **Crucifix** | When an entity is about to kill you or in unavoidable situations | On minor entities like Timothy or Eyes |
| **Lockpick** | When you see locked containers | When you have limited inventory space and no containers nearby |
| **Skeleton Key** | On locked doors when you suspect a shop is behind | On every locked door (save for guaranteed shop) |
| **Shears** | When Bramble blocks your path | Not needed if you have no Bramble encounters |
| **Alarm Clock** | During The Nest (Door 200) to distract Figure | During regular Figure encounters (less effective) |
| **Glowstick** | In Gloombat-heavy dark rooms | In well-lit areas (unnecessary) |
| **Barrel of Starlight** | As an absolute last resort against powerful entities | Early in the run (save for emergencies) |

## ITEM FAQ

**Q: What is the most important item to buy first?**
A: The **Lighter** (100g) is the best first purchase. Dark rooms and Screech are immediate threats, and 100g is easy to accumulate early in a run.

**Q: Should I save gold or spend it immediately?**
A: Spend on **essential items immediately** (Lighter, Bandages). Save for the Crucifix if you are accumulating gold and already have essentials.

**Q: Can I carry multiple of the same item?**
A: Yes, you can carry multiple copies of most items, but it is generally inefficient. One good light source is better than two cheap ones.

**Q: What happens to my items when I die?**
A: All in-run items and gold are lost when you die. Only permanent currencies (Knobs, Stardust) and out-of-run upgrades persist.

**Q: Is the Skeleton Key worth 300g?**
A: Yes, if you can afford it. Using a Skeleton Key on a locked door guarantees Jeff's Shop spawns, which can be run-changing. It also opens Skull Locks.

**Q: How many Alarm Clocks do I need for The Nest?**
A: Carry at least **2-3 Alarm Clocks** to effectively distract Figure during the Door 200 Anchor activation sequence.

**Q: Can I use items during entity encounters?**
A: Yes. You can use Vitamins during a Seek chase, throw an Alarm Clock during a Figure encounter, or activate a Crucifix when an entity is about to hit you.

**Q: What is the Barrel of Starlight?**
A: It is a rare, powerful banishment item found in containers. Use it as an absolute last resort against entities that are about to kill you.

**Q: Do items stack in inventory?**
A: Consumable items like Bandages and Vitamins stack. Unique items like the Crucifix and Skeleton Key take individual inventory slots.
