## What Is DOORS Speedrunning?

DOORS speedrunning is the practice of completing DOORS floors in the shortest possible time. Unlike casual play where exploration, item collection, and achievement hunting take priority, speedrunning focuses on **minimizing time spent on every single element** -- from door transitions to entity encounters to puzzle solving.

The DOORS speedrunning community is active and competitive, with runs tracked across multiple categories and floors. Whether you're aiming to beat your personal best or compete for community records, this guide covers everything you need to know.

### Speedrun Categories

For general gameplay fundamentals before attempting speedruns, see our [DOORS Beginner Guide](/posts/doors/beginner-guide/).

| Category | Definition | Time Target (Hotel) |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Any%** | Reach the final door by any means necessary | Under 12 minutes |
| **100%** | Complete all objectives, find all secrets, encounter all entities | Under 40 minutes |
| **No Damage** | Complete the floor without taking any damage | Under 20 minutes |
| **All Floors%** | Complete Hotel + Mines + Outdoors in one run | Under 55 minutes |
| **Individual Floor** | Speedrun a single floor (Hotel, Mines, or Outdoors) | Varies by floor |

### Speedrun Rules and Standards

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Start time** | Timer starts when you enter the first door |
| **End time** | Timer stops when you enter the final door of the floor |
| **Allowed** | Route optimization, entity skips, puzzle speed strategies |
| **Disallowed** | Exploits, glitches, external tools, teaming with non-runners |
| **Platform** | PC recommended for fastest input response |

**Library speed strategy (Door 50):**

2. Collect only the required books -- ignore extras

4. Enter code immediately -- practice the number combinations

## Entity Skip Strategies

Speedrunning DOORS means minimizing time spent on entity encounters. Here are proven strategies for handling each entity type at speed:

### Rush and Ambush

| Strategy | Execution | Time Saved |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Pre-knowledge of hiding spots** | Memorize locker/cabinet locations per room | 2-3 seconds per encounter |
| **Minimum hide time** | Exit hiding spot the instant the entity passes | 1-2 seconds per encounter |
| **Ambush multi-pass awareness** | Know the exact number of passes; don't over-hide | 3-5 seconds per encounter |

**Rush speed technique:** Instead of running to the nearest hiding spot when lights flicker, position yourself near a hiding spot at all times. This eliminates the "run to cover" delay.

### Screech

| Strategy | Execution | Time Saved |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Instant camera spin** | As soon as you hear whispering, spin once and keep moving | 1-2 seconds |
| **Preventive awareness** | In dark rooms, spin camera preemptively | Avoids Screech encounter entirely |

### Seek

| Strategy | Execution | Time Saved |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Memorized chase route** | Know the exact path, jump points, and obstacle locations | 10-20 seconds per chase |
| **Optimized sprint timing** | Sprint at every possible moment; never let off the key | 5-10 seconds per chase |
| **Jump pre-timing** | Jump before reaching the obstacle, not at it | 2-3 seconds per obstacle |

### Figure

| Strategy | Execution | Time Saved |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Pre-planned route** | Know the layout before Figure activates | 15-30 seconds |
| **Optimal crouch speed** | Crouch-walk at maximum speed (not minimum) | 5-10 seconds |
| **Alarm Clock placement** | Place clock before engaging; let it distract Figure | 20-40 seconds |

### Dupe

| Strategy | Execution | Time Saved |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Instant door count** | Count doors on entry; enter correct one immediately | 2-3 seconds |
| **No-hesitation policy** | Don't second-guess; commit to the correct door | 1-2 seconds |

### Giggle (Mines)

| Strategy | Execution | Time Saved |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Preemptive ceiling check** | Look up on room entry; move away immediately | 1-2 seconds |
| **Position awareness** | Don't stand in open areas; stay near cover | Avoids Giggle encounter entirely |

### Gloombats (Mines)

| Strategy | Execution | Time Saved |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Always have light on** | Carry active light source; no need to equip mid-encounter | Avoids encounter entirely |
| **Glowstick toss** | Pre-toss glowstick in dark areas before entering | 2-3 seconds |

### Groundskeeper (Outdoors)

| Strategy | Execution | Time Saved |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Memorized patrol patterns** | Know when and where Groundskeeper looks | 10-20 seconds per encounter |
| **Path optimization** | Plan routes that minimize grass proximity | 5-10 seconds per section |
| **Cross during blind spots** | Move quickly during Groundskeeper's non-watching phases | 5-15 seconds |

## Common Speedrun Mistakes

| Mistake | Time Lost | Prevention |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| **Over-hiding from Rush/Ambush** | 3-5 seconds per encounter | Learn exact pass duration; unhide immediately |
| **Wrong door on Dupe** | 10-20 seconds | Count doors on entry; commit instantly |
| **Fumbling puzzle inputs** | 15-30 seconds | Practice puzzles in casual runs first |
| **Suboptimal container checks** | 10-20 seconds | Only check containers with known valuable spawns |
| **Poor Inventory management** | 5-10 seconds | Pre-sort inventory; know what to drop instantly |
| **Bad petal collection order (Outdoors)** | 1-2 minutes | Memorize optimal petal route before speedrunning |
| **Nest Anchor route errors (Mines)** | 30-60 seconds | Practice the Nest route in casual runs |
| **Stepping on grass during Outdoors speedrun** | Instant death (reset) | Prioritize safety over speed near grass |
| **Not adapting to bad RNG** | 2-5 minutes | Have backup strategies for missing key items |
| **Panic during entity encounters** | 5-15 seconds | Practice entity handling until it's automatic |

### The Most Common Speedrun-Killer

**Panic during entity encounters** is the single biggest time-waster for new speedrunners. When Rush triggers or Figure appears, the instinct to rush (pun intended) causes mistakes. The fix is **repetition** -- practice each entity encounter until your response is automatic, not panicked.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the fastest way to beat The Hotel?**
A: The **Any% speedrun** focuses on sprinting through all doors, minimizing entity hide times, skipping non-essential containers, and solving puzzles at maximum speed. Top runners complete The Hotel in under 10 minutes.

**Q: How do I practice for DOORS speedrunning?**
A: Start by learning all room layouts and entity patterns through casual runs. Then practice individual sections (puzzles, entity encounters) before combining everything into full speed runs. Use a timer tool like LiveSplit to track your splits.

**Q: What's the hardest floor to speedrun?**
A: **The Mines** is generally considered the hardest due to generator rooms, the Nest boss fight requiring precise Alarm Clock timing, and the higher entity density. The Outdoors follows closely due to the Groundskeeper's instant-death mechanic.

**Q: Can I speedrun DOORS with friends?**
A: Multiplayer speedruns are possible but typically slower due to coordination overhead. Most community records are set in solo runs. However, co-op runs can be fun for practice.

**Q: What items should I prioritize in a speedrun?**
A: **Hotel:** Lighter, Vitamins. **Mines:** Shears, Alarm Clocks, Crowbar (if found). **Outdoors:** Lotus Petals, Vitamins for the escape. Skip everything else unless it directly saves time.

**Q: How do I deal with bad RNG in speedruns?**
A: Have backup strategies. If you don't find a Crowbar, optimize your generator room route. If entity spawns are unfavorable, adjust your hiding strategy. Good speedrunners adapt to any situation.

**Q: Where are DOORS speedrun records tracked?**
A: Community records are tracked on **speedrun.com** under the DOORS category, in the official DOORS Discord server, and through YouTube video submissions. Times are self-reported but community-verified.

**Q: What's the difference between Any% and 100% speedruns?**
A: **Any%** focuses on reaching the final door by any means, skipping all optional content. **100%** requires finding all secrets, encountering all entities, and completing all objectives. 100% runs are significantly longer.

**Q: Is the No Damage category viable?**
A: Yes, but it's much slower than Any%. You need to prioritize safety over speed, which means longer hiding times, more cautious navigation, and avoiding all risky skips. Top No Damage Hotel runs are around 14 minutes.

**Q: What's the best way to improve my split times?**
A: Focus on your **weakest section first**. Use a timer to identify which doors take you the longest, then practice that specific section repeatedly. Small improvements across many sections add up to major total time savings.
