## What Is Pressure?

**Pressure** is a first-person horror game on Roblox developed by **Urbanshade: Hadal Division**. Inspired by the popular game DOORS, you play as a prisoner sent into the **Hadal Blacksite** — a classified underwater monster research facility — to retrieve **Z-2 "The Crystal"** from the heart of the base and reach the extraction point.

Unlike DOORS, Pressure features its own unique entity roster, item system, and room generation mechanics. The game has over **115 doors** to progress through, each presenting new hazards, entities, and puzzles.

**Why play Pressure?**
- Tense, atmospheric horror with unique entity encounters
- Deep progression system with items and keycards to discover
- Multiplayer co-op: you can save teammates from deadly situations
- Regular updates adding new entities, rooms, and modifiers
- 42+ documents to collect for lore enthusiasts

## Getting Started: Your First Run

### Understand the Goal

Your mission is simple: **reach Room 100**, retrieve the crystal, and escape through the extraction point. Each door you open advances you further into the facility. There is no time limit — take your time and explore every room thoroughly.

### Learn the Core Loop

Each room in the Hadal Blacksite follows a basic pattern:

1. **Enter the room** through the door behind you
2. **Search for items** — flashlights, keycards, bandages, and other equipment
3. **Watch for entity warnings** — flickering lights, audio cues, environmental changes
4. **Survive the encounter** — use lockers, hide, or run depending on the entity
5. **Open the next door** — find the door handle/panel and proceed

### Essential Items to Grab First

| Item | Why You Need It | Where to Find It |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Flashlight** | See in dark rooms, essential for survival | Starting room, desks, drawers |
| **Keycard** | Unlock locked doors and panels | Hidden in rooms, on tables, in cabinets |
| **Bandages** | Heal after entity encounters | Medical rooms, supply closets |
| **Glowstick** | Light source that doesn't need batteries | Storage rooms, desks |

> **Pro Tip:** Items have a faint glow that makes them visible in dark rooms. If you're struggling to find items, scan the room slowly and look for anything that shines.

## Entity Survival Guide

Entities are the primary threat in Pressure. Each one has unique warning signs and survival mechanics. For a complete bestiary, see the [Entities Guide](/posts/pressure/entities-guide/). Here's how to handle the most common encounters.

### Angler (Z-283)

**The entity you'll encounter first and most often.**

| Warning | What to Do | What NOT to Do |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| Lights flicker on and off | Stay calm, locate the nearest locker | Don't rush into the first locker you see |
| Distant splashing sound | Wait until the sound gets close | Don't hide too early — the Angler checks empty lockers |
| Water dripping from ceiling | Enter locker and close the door | Don't peek until the lights stop flickering |

**Survival Strategy:** The Angler is a test of patience. When the lights start flickering, **locate a locker but don't enter yet**. Wait until you hear the splashing sound get close, then quickly enter and close the door. The Angler will check nearby lockers — if you entered too early, it will find you.

### Pinkie

**A stealth entity that doesn't give obvious warnings.**

| Warning | What to Do | What NOT to Do |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| No light flickering | Stay near a locker at all times | Don't assume you're safe just because the lights are steady |
| Subtle visual distortion | Enter locker immediately | Don't wait for a "clear" signal — there isn't one |
| Quiet breathing sound | Close the door and wait | Don't move around while Pinkie is nearby |

**Survival Strategy:** Pinkie is dangerous because it doesn't flicker lights like the Angler. Your best defense is to **always stay within a few steps of a locker**. When you notice subtle environmental changes (screen distortion, unusual sounds), enter the nearest locker immediately.

### Blitz

**A fast rush entity with minimal warning.**

| Warning | What to Do | What NOT to Do |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| Sudden screen shake | Sprint to the nearest locker | Don't stand still and try to figure out what's happening |
| Loud roaring sound | Enter locker, close door, wait | Don't try to outrun it — it's faster than you |
| Red-tinted lighting | Same as above | Don't peek during the rush |

**Survival Strategy:** Blitz is all about reaction speed. The warning is short — screen shake and a roar. As soon as you notice either, **drop everything and get into a locker**. Don't try to save items or finish searching the room.

### A-60 (Multi-Monster)

**A multi-phase entity that sweeps through multiple rooms.**

| Warning | What to Do | What NOT to Do |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| Repeating audio cue (every few seconds) | Find a locker and prepare to wait | Don't assume one pass is the end |
| Growing intensity of sound | Enter locker on first cue | Don't wait for the "big" warning |
| Multiple sweep patterns | Hide, wait, hide again if needed | Don't exit between sweeps |

**Survival Strategy:** A-60 doesn't just pass once — it may sweep through multiple times. Enter a locker on the first audio cue and **wait through all sweeps** before exiting. The key is patience: don't peek between passes.

### A-200 (Multi-Phase Rush)

**A slower but more dangerous multi-phase entity.**

| Warning | What to Do | What NOT to Do |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| Deep rumbling sound | Find cover immediately | Don't ignore it thinking it's far away |
| Progressive environmental changes | Stay hidden through all phases | Don't exit after the first phase ends |
| Multiple visual distortions | Wait for all warnings to stop | Don't move during any phase |

**Survival Strategy:** A-200 has extended multi-phase patterns. Each phase has its own warning, and the entity gets more aggressive with each pass. Stay hidden until you're absolutely certain all phases have completed.

### ChainSmoker

**An entity tied to specific path mechanics.**

| Warning | What to Do | What NOT to Do |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| Smoke/fog filling the room | Look for visual path indicators | Don't blindly run through the smoke |
| Specific room triggers | Follow the safe path markers | Don't linger in smoky areas |
| Coughing/choking effects | Move quickly along the safe route | Don't backtrack through smoke |

**Survival Strategy:** ChainSmoker fills rooms with hazardous smoke. Look for visual markers (often subtle light or color differences) that indicate the safe path through the room. Move quickly but carefully — the smoke damages you over time.

The Hadal Blacksite generates rooms procedurally, but there are recognizable room types. For a complete floor-by-floor walkthrough, see the [Floors and Locations Guide](/posts/pressure/floors-and-locations-guide/).

| Room Type | Frequency | Key Features | Tips |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|------|
| **Standard Corridor** | Very Common | Basic hallway with a door | Search desks and cabinets for items |
| **Supply Room** | Common | Storage shelves, crates, lockers | Good source of bandages and glowsticks |
| **Medical Bay** | Uncommon | Medical equipment, first aid supplies | Always check for bandages and keycards |
| **Heavy Containment** | Rare (early), Common (late) | High-security cells, dangerous entities | Prepare for tough encounters — have a locker ready |
| **Flesh Room** | Rare | Organic, mutated environment | Unpredictable entity spawns — stay alert |
| **Locker Room** | Uncommon | Multiple lockers in one area | Safe zone — but don't get complacent |
| **Firewall Section** | Uncommon | Gaps in the floor, hazardous drops | Watch your step — falling means instant death |
| **Crystal Room** | Once per run (Room 100) | The Crystal — main objective | Grab it and head to extraction |

## Proven Strategies for Reaching Room 100

### Strategy 1: The Methodical Explorer

**Best for:** First-time players, single-player runs

1. Enter each room and immediately locate all lockers
2. Search every desk, cabinet, and shelf for items
3. Only open the next door when you're sure you've found everything
4. Always have a flashlight equipped before entering new rooms
5. Keep at least 1 bandage in reserve

**Why it works:** You'll never be caught without essential items, and knowing the room layout before entities appear gives you a crucial advantage.

### Strategy 2: The Team Player

**Best for:** Multiplayer groups

1. Assign roles: one searcher, one lookout, one item carrier
2. Use voice communication to call out entity warnings
3. Share items equally — everyone needs a flashlight
4. Never leave a teammate behind during entity encounters

**Why it works:** Coordination multiplies your survival chances. A 4-player team with good communication can handle encounters that would kill a solo player.

## Common Beginner Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | How to Avoid |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| **Rushing through rooms** | Miss items, walk into entities unprepared | Search thoroughly, listen for cues |
| **Hiding in lockers too early** | Angler and other entities check empty lockers | Wait until the entity is close before hiding |
| **Ignoring audio cues** | Miss entity warnings entirely | Play with sound on — it's essential |
| **Running out of flashlight batteries** | Lost in dark rooms, can't see entities | Carry a glowstick or lantern as backup |
| **Forgetting to pick up keycards** | Can't progress past locked doors | Check every surface for keycards |
| **Falling in Firewall sections** | Instant death | Watch the floor carefully, move slowly |
| **Peeking too early after entity passes** | Entity returns and catches you | Wait for ALL warnings to stop before exiting |

## FAQ

**Q: Is Pressure free to play?**
A: Yes, Pressure is free on Roblox.

**Q: How many doors are there in total?**
A: There are over 115 doors to progress through, with Room 100 being the main objective (the crystal room).

**Q: What's the hardest entity for beginners?**
A: **Pinkie** is often cited as the hardest for new players because it doesn't flicker lights or give obvious warnings. Unlike the Angler, you need to rely on subtle environmental cues and always stay near a locker.

**Q: Can I play Pressure with friends?**
A: Yes! Pressure supports multiplayer. You can team up with friends and even save them from deadly situations by pulling them out of lockers.

**Q: What are the 42 documents?**
A: Documents are collectible lore items scattered throughout the Hadal Blacksite. Each document reveals redacted information about the facility, the entities, and the Z-2 Crystal. There are 42 documents in total, and collecting them all unlocks additional lore about the game's story.

**Q: What are modifiers?**
A: Modifiers are gameplay changes you can enable for additional challenge or variety. There are 55+ modifiers available, ranging from faster entity speeds to altered room generation. They're optional and great for replayability.

**Q: How do I survive the Firewall sections?**
A: Firewall sections have gaps in the floor that cause instant death if you fall. Walk slowly, watch where you step, and look for visual cues that indicate safe flooring. Don't run — the time saved isn't worth the risk.
