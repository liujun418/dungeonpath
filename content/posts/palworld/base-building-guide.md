## Base Building Fundamentals

Your base is the heart of your Palworld operation. It serves as your safe haven, production hub, and Pal management center. Understanding the core mechanics is essential before you start laying foundations.

### Base Stats

| Stat | Starting Value | Maximum | How to Upgrade |
|------|---------------|---------|---------------|
| Pal capacity | 10 | 20 | Upgrade Palbox to level 3 |
| Base area | Default circle | Same | Cannot expand size, choose location carefully |
| Number of bases | 1 | 3 | Level 10 (2nd base), Level 20 (3rd base) |
| Maximum structures | ~200 per base | ~200 | Hard cap |
| Raid frequency | Every 30-60 min | Variable | Increases with base level |

### Palbox Levels

Your Palbox can be upgraded using materials. Higher levels unlock more Pal slots and better base capabilities.

| Palbox Level | Pal slots | Upgrade Materials | Unlock Requirements |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 1 | 10 | None (built at level 2) | Level 2 technology |
| 2 | 12 | 30x Wood, 20x Stone, 10x Paldium | Level 5 |
| 3 | 15 | 50x Wood, 30x Stone, 20x Paldium, 10x Ingot | Level 10 |
| 4 | 17 | 50x Refined Ingot, 30x Paldium, 10x Ancient Parts | Level 20 |
| 5 | 20 | 100x Refined Ingot, 50x Paldium, 20x Ancient Parts, 5x Electric Organ | Level 35 |

**Priority:** Rush Palbox level 3 (15 Pals) by level 10. Then focus on getting to level 4 by level 20.

## Optimal Base Layout Design

### The 3-Base Strategy

You can place three bases total. Do not waste them on the same resource type.

| Base Slot | Purpose | Ideal Location | Key Buildings |
|-----------|---------|---------------|--------------|
| Base 1 | Main Hub / Production | Grasslands (central) | Palbox, crafting stations, breeding pen, storage, farm |
| Base 2 | Mining / Ore Operation | Near ore cluster (Plateau) | Palbox, Furnace, mining stations, chests |
| Base 3 | Specialized (Quartz/Sulfur/Breeding) | Appropriate biome | Based on purpose |

### Layout Design Principles

**Principle 1: Flat ground only.** Even a 1-degree slope can break Pal pathfinding. Scout thoroughly before placing your Palbox.

**Principle 2: Keep workstations close together.** Place your crafting area (Workbench, Sphere Factory, Weapon Workbench) within a 3x3 foundation area. Pals travel shorter = faster production.

**Principle 3: Separate food production from crafting.** Place Berry/Wheat plantations in a separate area of the base where Watering and Planting Pals work without interfering with Handiwork Pals.

**Principle 4: Defensive perimeter.** Build a wall around your base (Stone, not Wood -- wood burns during fire raids).

### Optimal 15-Pal Base Layout

```
Area 1: Crafting Hub (3x4 foundations)
  - 2x Workbench
  - 1x Sphere Factory
  - 1x Weapon Workbench
  - 1x Armor Workbench
  - 1x Repair Bench
  - Anubis assigned here (Handiwork 4)

Area 2: Food Production (4x4 foundations)  
  - 3x Berry Plantation (or 2x Wheat + 2x Berry)
  - 1x Mill
  - 1x Cooking Pot
  - 1x Cooler Box
  - Jormuntide (Watering) + Lyleen (Planting) assigned here

Area 3: Processing (2x2 foundations)
  - 1x Furnace (or 2x for smelting)
  - 1x Refined Furnace
  - 1x Electric Furnace (later)
  - Jormuntide Ignis (Kindling) assigned here

Area 4: Ranch (2x2 area)
  - 2x Ranch (one for Lamball/Mozzarina, one for Beegarde)
  - Chikipi, Mozzarina, Beegarde assigned

Area 5: Breeding
  - 1x Breeding Pen
  - Cake box nearby

Area 6: Pal Care
  - 1x Hot Spring (Sanity recovery)
  - 1x Pal Beds (one per Pal)
  - Feed Box (central location)

Area 7: Storage
  - 4-6x Large Chests clustered together
  - One chest per resource type (Wood/Stone/Metal separate)
```

## Automation Setup

### Food Automation (Self-Sustaining)

1. **Plantation -> Water -> Harvest -> Cook** cycle:

| Step | Building | Pal Needed | Output |
|------|----------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Berry Plantation | Planter (Tanzee/Lyleen) | Raw berries grow |
| 2 | Water | Waterer (Jormuntide/Penking) | Crops grow |
| 3 | Harvest | Gatherer (Tanzee/Petallia) | Berries go to Feed Box |
| 4 | Cook | Kindler (Jormuntide Ignis/Foxsparks) | Cooked/Baked berries in Feed Box |
| 5 | Eat | Any base Pal | Pals feed themselves |

**Best food chain progression:**
- Level 5-15: Berry Plantation + Cooked Berries
- Level 15-30: Add Wheat Plantation -> Mill -> Bread
- Level 30-40: Add Tomato + Lettuce plantations -> Salad (best worker food)
- Level 40+: Add Mozzarina Milk + Wheat + Tomato -> Pizza (best overall food)

### Ore Mining Automation

1. Build your base near an ore cluster (3+ ore nodes within base boundary).
2. Assign a Miner (Digtoise or Astegon).
3. Place chests near the mining area for Transport Pals to stockpile.
4. Transport Pals automatically move mined ore from the ground to chests.

**Optimal ore base location:** The plateau south of Rayne Tower has 5+ ore nodes within base range.

### Fuel Automation (Charcoal)

For Refined Ingots, you need Charcoal:

1. Build a **Furnace** (or Refined Furnace).
2. Assign a Kindling Pal (preferably Jormuntide Ignis).
3. Insert Wood -> the Furnace automatically produces Charcoal.
4. Charcoal + Ore = Refined Ingots.

**Ratio:** 1 Wood produces 1 Charcoal. 2 Charcoal + 2 Ore = 1 Refined Ingot.

### Sphere Production Automation

1. Assign a Handiwork Pal (Anubis pref.) near a Sphere Factory.
2. Keep Paldium + Ingots in nearby chests.
3. The Pal auto-crafts spheres.
4. Endgame: Use Assembly Lines for mass production.

| Sphere Type | Materials | Assembly Line | Requires |
|-------------|-----------|--------------|----------|
| Pal Sphere | 3 Wood, 3 Stone, 1 Paldium | No | Level 2 |
| Mega Sphere | 4 Wood, 4 Stone, 2 Paldium, 1 Ingot | No | Level 14 |
| Giga Sphere | 6 Wood, 6 Stone, 3 Paldium, 2 Ingot, 1 Refined Ingot | No (or Assembly Line) | Level 25 |
| Hyper Sphere | 5 Paldium, 5 Ingot, 3 Refined Ingot, 2 Carbon Fiber | Assembly Line I | Level 32 |
| Ultra Sphere | 7 Paldium, 7 Ingot, 5 Refined Ingot, 4 Carbon Fiber, 1 Circuit Board | Assembly Line II | Level 40 |
| Legendary Sphere | 10 Paldium, 10 Ingot, 5 Refined Ingot, 5 Carbon Fiber, 2 Circuit Board | Assembly Line II | Level 50 |

## Base Defense (Raid Management)

Raids occur periodically where hostile Pals or human NPCs attack your base. For dedicated boss fight strategies, see the [Boss & Tower Guide](../palworld/boss-and-tower-guide.md).

### Raid Mechanics

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Frequency | Every 30-60 minutes of gameplay |
| Difficulty | Scales with base level and player level |
| Composition | Based on base location biome |
| Target | They attack your Palbox primarily |
| Reward | XP, drops from defeated raiders |
| Opt-out | Building in remote locations reduces raid frequency |

### Defense Strategy

**Phase 1: Wood Walls (Level 2-10)**
Simple wooden walls around the perimeter. These WILL burn if fire-type Pals raid you. Acceptable only for the first few hours.

**Phase 2: Stone Walls (Level 13-30)**
Upgrade to stone walls as soon as you unlock the **Stone Foundation** technology (level 13). Stone is fireproof and significantly stronger.

**Phase 3: Metal Walls + Defenses (Level 30-50)**
Use Metal Walls (Refined Ingot) and add defensive structures:

| Defense | Level | Materials | Effect |
|---------|-------|-----------|--------|
| Wooden Spike Trap | 7 | 30 Wood | Damages enemies walking over it |
| Stone Spike Trap | 15 | 50 Stone | Better damage, more durable |
| Mounted Crossbow | 21 | 30 Ingot, 30 Wood, 10 Nail | Auto-targets raid enemies |
| Electric Fence | 30 | 50 Ingot, 10 Electric Organ | Stuns enemies in range |
| Mounted Musket | 34 | 50 Refined Ingot, 50 Wood, 20 Gunpowder | High damage auto-turret |
| Mounted Shotgun | 40 | 80 Refined Ingot, 30 Polymer | Highest damage turret |

### Defense Placement Tips

1. Build a **wall 3 blocks high** around the base perimeter.
2. Place **defensive mounts** on elevated platforms behind walls.
3. Leave only one gap in the wall (controlled entrance) lined with spike traps.
4. Keep strong **combat Pals** in your party, not your base -- they don't defend the base while in your team.

## Pal Happiness and Sanity Management

Pals have a hidden **Sanity** stat that degrades while they work and recovers while they sleep, bathe, or eat well.

### Sanity Drains

| Activity | Sanity Loss |
|----------|------------|
| Working (per hour) | Medium |
| Working at night (if not Nocturnal) | High |
| Working without sleep (deprived) | Very High |
| Getting injured in a raid | High |
| Eating raw/low-quality food | Small |
| Overcrowding (more Pals than beds) | Medium |
| Depressed state (sick) | Continuous drain |

### Sanity Recovery Methods

| Method | Recovery Rate | Level Required | Notes |
|--------|--------------|---------------|-------|
| Pal Bed (Straw) | Slow | 3 | Bare minimum, acceptable early game |
| Pal Bed (Fluffy) | Moderate | 19 | Better recovery rate |
| Pal Bed (Premium) | Fast | 38 | Best beds for work efficiency |
| Hot Spring | Fast | 12 | Multiple Pals can use simultaneously |
| High-quality food | Moderate+ | Varies | Salad, Pizza restore significant Sanity |
| Medicine | Instant | 20+ | Low Grade Medical Supplies heal Depression |

**Optimal Sanity Management Setup:**
- 1 Premium Pal Bed per Pal (level 38)
- 1 Hot Spring (level 12) -- place near work area
- Salad or Pizza in the Feed Box (level 36+)

### Sick Pals

If a Pal's Sanity reaches zero, it develops **Depression** and stops working. You must:
1. Use **Low Grade Medical Supplies** or **High Grade Medical Supplies** to cure them.
2. Build better beds and a Hot Spring.
3. Improve food quality.

Check the Medicine tab in the Technology tree at level 20 for medical supply crafting.

## Multiple Base Strategy

At level 10 you unlock a second Palbox, and at level 20 you unlock a third. This is your most important power spike for resource production.

### Base 1: Main Hub (Central Grasslands)

**Purpose:** Primary crafting, storage, breeding, farming.

**Key buildings:**
- Palbox (highest level available)
- 3x Berry/Wheat/Vegetable plantations
- Ranch (Chikipi, Mozzarina, Beegarde)
- Breeding Pen
- All crafting stations (Workbench, Sphere Factory, Weapon Bench, Armor Bench)
- 4+ Large Chests
- Furnace, Refined Furnace, Electric Furnace
- Cooking Pot, Cooler Box
- Hot Spring, Premium Beds

### Base 2: Mining Operation (Near Ore)

**Purpose:** Ore, coal, sulfur, stone production.

**Key buildings:**
- Palbox (level 2 minimum)
- Furnace (smelt on-site)
- 6+ Large Chests (ore storage)
- Pal Beds + Hot Spring
- Assign: 2-3 Mining Pals (Astegon/Digtoise), 2 Transport Pals (Wumpo/Cattiva)

**Best locations:** Plateau south of Rayne Tower, Grassy Behemoth Hills.

### Base 3: Specialized Resource Farm

**Options for Base 3:**

| Strategy | Location | Resources | Pals Needed |
|----------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Quartz Mining | Icy Weald or Frozen Lake | Quartz, Coal | Mining Pals (Astegon) |
| Sulfur Mining | Volcanic Foothills | Sulfur, Coal | Mining Pals + Heat resist gear |
| Pure Breeding Base | Any flat area | Nothing specific | Breeding Pen + Cake farm |
| Ranch Mega-farm | Grasslands | All ranch resources | 4x Ranch + all ranch Pals |
| Oil Extraction | Coast (Sakurajima) | Crude Oil | Oil rig + Electric Generator |

### Base Transfer Logistics

You can fast travel between bases using the Palbox map menu. Resources are shared across bases through your own inventory -- there is no cross-base resource network. You must physically carry items between bases. Use a flying mount (Jetragon, Frostallion) or grapple gun + Galeclaw glider for fast transit.

**Pro tip:** Build a chest at each base labeled "Transfer" and dump overflow resources there. When you fly to your other base, grab everything from the Transfer chest and bring it to the main base.
